Leçon 1 – The Passive Voice

La voix passive — Construction et usage en contexte

📖 Cours — Formation

Structure de base

Subject + to be (conjugué) + past participle (+ by + agent)
TempsFormuleExemple
Present simpleis/are + PPSlaves are sold.
Past simplewas/were + PPHarriet was born in 1820.
Present perfecthas/have been + PPShe has been captured.
Modal (can/must…)modal + be + PPSlaves must be obeyed.
💡 Quand utiliser la voix passive ? Quand l'agent (qui fait l'action) est inconnu, sans importance, ou qu'on veut mettre l'objet en avant.
"Cotton was picked on plantations." (on ne sait pas / peu importe qui)
Active → Passive Active : The master whipped the slave.
Passive : The slave was whipped by the master.

Leçon 2 – Had to / Didn't have to

L'obligation passée — Affirmation, négation, question

📖 Cours — Had to
Obligation passée : Subject + had to + base verb Absence d'obligation : Subject + didn't have to + base verb Question : Did + subject + have to + base verb ?
SensExemple
had toétait obligé de (obligation)Slaves had to obey their masters.
didn't have ton'était pas obligé deFree men didn't have to work for planters.
had to + passifdevait être + PPCotton had to be picked every day.
💡 Had to ≠ Must Must = obligation présente. Had to = obligation PASSÉE.
Pas de "musted" en anglais → on utilise toujours had to au passé !
Contexte esclavage "Slaves had to work from sunrise to sunset."
"They didn't have to be paid."
"Did they have to live in cabins?" → "Yes, they did."

Leçon 3 – Harriet Tubman

Biographie · Vie sur la plantation · Underground Railroad

📖 Cours — Frise chronologique
1821 or 1823
Born into slavery in Maryland as Araminta Ross. The exact date is unknown.
1826 or 1827
Loaned / rented to another master at the age of 5. She had to look after her master's children.
Childhood
Lived in a one-room cabin with her family. Life was hard on the plantation. She was often beaten and whipped by her cruel master.
1849
Escaped and fled to Philadelphia — a Northern free state. Left her family behind.
1850–1860
Conducted the Underground Railroad — helped ~300 people escape, including her own family. Never lost a single passenger.
1861–65
Worked as a spy and nurse during the Civil War.
1913
Died — nicknamed "Moses" and "The Black Moses".
🌾 Vie sur la plantation — Key facts

🏚 Living conditions

She lived in a one-room cabin with her whole family.
Cabins were small, dark and cold.
"Living conditions were very poor."

😢 Daily life

She had to work hard in the fields.
She had to look after her master's children.
She was beaten and whipped regularly.
Her master was cruel.
⚠️ Dates à retenir (deux versions possibles) Naissance : 1821 or 1823 — les deux sont acceptées, les archives de l'époque sont imprécises.
Louée à un autre maître : 1826 or 1827 (she was about 5 years old).
Phrases clés à connaître pour le contrôle "She was born into slavery in Maryland in 1821 (or 1823)."
"She was loaned/rented to another master when she was 5."
"She had to look after her master's children."
"Life was hard on the plantation — she was often beaten and whipped."
"She lived in a one-room cabin with her family."
"She escaped to Philadelphia in 1849."
"She helped about 300 people escape, including her own family."
"A reward was offered for her capture, but she was never caught."
"She was called Moses because she led her people to freedom."
🚂 Underground Railroad

Pas un vrai chemin de fer ! C'était un réseau secret de routes, maisons et personnes qui aidaient les esclaves à fuir vers le Nord ou le Canada. Harriet en était le "conductor" (guide).

💡 Chiffres clés ~300 personnes aidées à s'échapper (dont sa propre famille) · 13 missions · Jamais capturée · Récompense offerte pour son arrestation

Leçon 4 – Free States vs Slave States

L'Amérique divisée — Géographie, économie et contexte historique

📖 Cours — Deux Amériques

🟢 Free States (Nord)

L'esclavage y était aboli
Économie industrielle
Salaires versés aux ouvriers
États : Pennsylvania, New York, Ohio…
Harriet Tubman fuyait vers ces états

🔴 Slave States (Sud)

L'esclavage y était légal
Économie agricole (coton, tabac, canne à sucre)
Travail forcé sans salaire
États : Virginia, Georgia, Mississippi…
Plantations, overseers, slave hunters
📜 Fugitive Slave Act (1850) Cette loi obligeait les états libres à renvoyer les esclaves fugitifs vers le Sud. C'est pourquoi certains esclaves devaient fuir jusqu'au Canada pour être vraiment libres.
Vocabulaire clé "Slavery was legal in the South but abolished in the North."
"Cotton and tobacco were grown on large plantations."
"Free states didn't allow slave owners to keep their slaves."
